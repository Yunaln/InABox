How to make Green Tea?


Instructions

  • Boil water and let it cool for a few minutes to reach the desired temperature (175-185°F or 80-85°C).
  • Avoid using boiling water as it can make the tea taste bitter.
  • If using loose green tea leaves, place the leaves in a tea strainer or infuser.
  • If using a tea bag, simply place the bag in a cup.
  • Pour the hot water over the green tea leaves or tea bag.
  • Allow the tea to steep for about 2-3 minutes.
  • Adjust the steeping time based on your preference for a lighter or stronger flavor.
  • Once the desired steeping time has passed, remove the tea strainer or tea bag from the cup.
  • If desired, add honey or lemon to enhance the flavor of the green tea.
  • Give the tea a gentle stir to incorporate any added ingredients.
  • Your green tea is now ready to be enjoyed! Sip it slowly and savor the delicate flavors and aroma.

Ingredients
  • 1 teaspoon of loose green tea leaves or 1 green tea bag
  • 1 cup of hot water (about 175-185°F or 80-85°C)
  • Honey or lemon (optional, for taste)

Allergy Note

Green tea is generally safe for most people to consume. However, it does contain caffeine, so individuals who are sensitive to caffeine should consume it in moderation. Additionally, if you have any known allergies to tea leaves or related plants, it's important to consult with a healthcare professional before consuming green tea.
